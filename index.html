Welcome!

Welcome to my academic homepage! I am a theoretical physicist specializing in quantum optics and quantum interferometry. In July 2025, I completed my Ph. D. in Physics from the Department of Physics, Institute of Science, Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi, India. This page highlights my research, publications, and professional journey.

During my doctoral research, I developed theoretical frameworks to mitigate photon loss and improve the phase sensitivity of the Mach-Zehnder interferometer (MZI) and SU(1,1) interferometers. I systematically studied the role of squeezed Kerr states (SKS) and Schrödinger cat-like states in quantum interferometry and showed that these states offer enhanced phase estimation beyond the standard quantum limit, outperforming squeezed vacuum states across various energy regimes, especially in low-photon cases such as biological sensing and spectroscopy. We also proposed a dual-output detection scheme for a squeezing-assisted MZI, achieving sub-shot-noise sensitivity over a broad phase range.

Current Research Activities

My current research explores two-photon absorption (TPA) using non-degenerate two-mode squeezed states. We are investigating how quantum intensity correlations can enhance TPA precision while addressing photon loss and detection inefficiencies.

I am also investigating the orbital angular momentum (OAM) of light, with the aim of formulating novel protocols for the controlled generation of diverse OAM clusters. I look forward to continuing to bridge theoretical innovation and experimental feasibility, contributing to the development of practical, high-impact quantum sensing technologies.

Research Interests
